## Market Sizing and Growth
- Global gross travel bookings are projected to reach **$1.61T in 2024**, reflecting strong demand despite economic headwinds.
- Travel growth continues above pre-pandemic levels.
- 2024 shows strong growth in APAC, with steady gains in North America and Europe. The Middle East and LATAM continue to emerge but remain smaller than the three regional powerhouses.

### Channel Mix and Digital Adoption
- Digital travel adoption continues to rise, signaling a growing preference for online travel services.
- Channel mix is shown across supplier-direct online, OTA, and offline, with online penetration trending upward.
- OCR indicates **online gross bookings reach about $1.0T in 2024**, with **annual gains of roughly 6-9% through 2026**.

## State of Travel Funding
### Funding Levels
- 2023 travel startup funding: **$5.2B** (near a 10-year low).
- Through **3Q24**, funding sits at **$4.3B**, roughly on track to match 2023.
- 2024 shows signs of a potential investment reboot as travel-focused VC funds raise new capital.

### Funding by Region (3Q24, OCR values)
- North America: **$1.5B**
- Europe: **$1.2B**
- APAC: **$1.1B**
- Middle East: **$284M**

### Top Funded Verticals (US$M, OCR values)
- Ride-hailing: **$1,213M**
- Air: **$616M**
- Corporate travel: **$615M**
- Hotel and hostel: **$570M**

### Business Focus Split
- **B2B funding overtook B2C** for the first time, reaching **51% of total funding** as of 3Q24.
- The shift is likely driven by clear GenAI use cases in B2B (customer service, workflow automation, analytics).

### Funding Outlook (Qualitative)
- Investing reboot: new travel-focused VC funds raise capital, signaling renewed cycles in 2025.
- GenAI role expands: automation in customer service, dynamic pricing, and travel planning reduces reliance on traditional funding models.
- Continued B2B shift, especially in corporate travel and logistics.
- Debt funding for mobility grows as a non-dilutive alternative.
- Europe leads in regional funding growth.
- Market volatility persists even with strong travel demand.
