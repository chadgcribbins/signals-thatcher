# What Makes Acme, Acme?

## Brand Essence

**Acme Hotels** is a family-owned, generationally minded collection of culinary-driven luxury hotels. We are architecturally distinguished properties in the world's most remarkable food regions, where exceptional modern design meets absolute regional provenance. Each hotel is a refined, intimate space for food-obsessed travelers to learn, taste, and immerse themselves in the ingredients and traditions that define a place. We grow patiently, invest in long-term relationships, and build for decades rather than seasons. This is where architecture, landscape, and cuisine converge into an unforgettable experience.

## Brand DNA Framework

### Purpose
**To create the world's most extraordinary culinary destinations through long-term family stewardship, where architecture, landscape, and regional food culture unite.**

We exist because food tells the most authentic story of place, and the most sophisticated travelers seek meaningful connection to the regions they visit. Great architecture should honor landscape, great cuisine should honor terroir, and great hospitality should honor both.

### Mission
**Build a small, global family collection of architecturally exceptional hotels, each positioned in its continent's finest food region, offering immersive culinary experiences that celebrate absolute regional provenance, sustainable practices, and long-term community relationships.**

## Brand Pillars

### 1. **Architecture Embedded in Landscape**

Every Acme property is a purpose-built architectural statement inspired by Frank Lloyd Wright's philosophy of organic architecture—modern structures that emerge from and honor their natural surroundings. We commission significant contemporary architects to design buildings that use regional materials, respond to local climate and topography, and create profound connection between interior and exterior spaces.

**What this means in practice:**
- Purpose-built properties designed by notable contemporary architects
- Modern design language that respects and responds to landscape
- Regional materials sourced sustainably—stone, wood, glass from the locale
- Approximately 30 rooms per property, maintaining intimacy and exclusivity
- Architecture that becomes part of the experience, not just background
- Spaces designed for how people actually want to live—work, gather, retreat, dine
- Integration of indoor/outdoor living, natural light, views
- Design decisions made for longevity, not trends

**Why it matters:**
Great architecture elevates every moment. Our guests arrive, see the building emerge from the landscape, and immediately understand: this place is different. The attention to design signals the attention to every detail that follows.

### 2. **Regional Provenance, Absolute Transparency**

Every dish served at Acme properties features ingredients sourced from the region—not a marketing radius, but the genuine agricultural and culinary identity of the place. In the Cotswolds, you eat Cotswolds ingredients. In Tuscany, you eat Tuscan ingredients. In Sonoma, you eat what Sonoma grows. Every menu provides full provenance: the farm, the forager, the producer, the distance.

**What this means in practice:**
- Each property named after its region (Acme Cotswolds, Acme Tuscany, Acme Sonoma)
- Sourcing defined by regional food culture and agricultural boundaries, not arbitrary mileage
- 100% regional sourcing with exceptions only for items impossible to grow locally (coffee, certain spices, specialized wines)
- Every dish on every menu includes full provenance story—who grew it, where, when
- Menus shift constantly with seasonality and availability
- On-property production: kitchen gardens, beehives, greenhouses, foraging programs, preserving operations
- Direct relationships with farmers, foragers, fishermen, cheesemakers, bakers
- Multi-year commitments that prioritize growers and makers over short-term margins

**Why it matters:**
This isn't farm-to-table theater. This is verifiable, traceable, absolute commitment to place. Guests can visit the farms, meet the producers, taste the difference. It creates cuisine that tastes distinctly of where you are—impossible to replicate elsewhere.

### 3. **Culinary Immersion & Education**

Acme is a destination for people who love food enough to want to understand it deeply. We offer open kitchens, chef interactions, foraging expeditions, cooking classes, technique demonstrations, producer visits, and wine education. Our guests don't just eat exceptional food—they learn how it's grown, how it's prepared, why it tastes this way, what makes it special.

**What this means in practice:**
- Open kitchen design in main restaurant—guests see preparation
- Chef's counter seating for direct interaction with culinary team
- Daily optional experiences: foraging walks, kitchen garden tours, beekeeping demonstrations, cheese-making sessions, cooking classes
- Executive chef with creative autonomy leading property's culinary program
- Seasonal cooking workshops led by chefs and guest experts
- Producer meet-and-greets—farmers, foragers, makers sharing their stories
- Culinary library in each property with regional food/agriculture focus
- Food photography encouraged—spaces designed with visual documentation in mind

**Why it matters:**
Our guests are sophisticated food lovers who've eaten at great restaurants worldwide. What we offer that they can't get elsewhere is depth—the chance to truly understand a region's food culture, not just consume it. They leave educated, inspired, and connected.

### 4. **Multiple Dining Experiences Throughout the Day**

Food is woven through every moment of the day at Acme, not confined to a single restaurant. We create distinct spaces and dining formats for different times and moods—from morning conservatory breakfast to bistro lunch to evening fine dining to late-night wine bar. Each offers a different experience of regional cuisine and architecture.

**What this means in practice:**
- **The Conservatory:** Light-filled breakfast space, often with glass walls overlooking kitchen gardens or landscape. Buffet-style with daily foraged additions, exceptional coffee, fresh baking
- **The Bistro:** Casual lunch venue with outdoor terrace. Regional classics, seasonal salads, artisan sandwiches. Perfect for working lunch or post-hike meal
- **The Restaurant:** Main fine dining space, open kitchen, chef's counter. Tasting menu format highlighting regional ingredients with technical excellence. This is the evening centerpiece
- **The Wine Bar:** Intimate late-night space. Regional wine focus with international selections. Small plates, cheese and charcuterie, dessert wines. Where guests linger after dinner

**Why it matters:**
Different moments call for different experiences. Morning energy requires different space than evening sophistication. By creating distinct venues, we allow guests to inhabit the property differently throughout their stay—always eating exceptionally, but with variety and choice.

### 5. **Work-Life Integration in Nature**

Acme recognizes that modern luxury travelers, especially affluent 30-45 year old professionals, no longer separate work and leisure cleanly. We design for how people actually live—needing to take calls, check emails, collaborate with teams, but wanting to do it in beautiful, restorative settings surrounded by nature and excellent food.

**What this means in practice:**
- Exceptional WiFi infrastructure throughout property and grounds
- Beautiful work-friendly spaces beyond guest rooms—library desks, outdoor tables, private nooks
- Corporate retreat packages: full property buyouts for leadership teams, board meetings, strategic planning
- Team-building through culinary experiences—group cooking classes, foraging expeditions, harvest dinners
- Meeting rooms with exceptional design, AV technology, natural light
- "Workation" positioning: extend the weekend, work remotely from beauty
- Rooms designed with proper desk space, excellent task lighting, ergonomic seating
- Zoom-ready spaces with good acoustics and backgrounds

**Why it matters:**
We don't pretend people disconnect completely. Instead, we create an environment where work happens effortlessly, leaving energy and time for the experiences that restore: hiking, foraging, cooking, eating, exploring. Companies bring teams here not for generic conference rooms, but for the combination of functional excellence and inspiring environment.

### 6. **Small Global Collection, Deep Local Roots**

Acme is building a small collection of properties across continents, each positioned in that region's finest food-growing territory within proximity to major airports. Every property shares architectural ambition, culinary commitment, and operational excellence—but each is distinctly itself, shaped by local landscape, ingredients, culture, and design cues. We grow only when we can steward a place for the long term, with family-level care and community trust.

**What this means in practice:**
- Portfolio goal: A limited number of properties worldwide, added patiently and selectively
- Location selection criteria: exceptional food region + natural beauty + airport access + buildable site
- Potential regions: Cotswolds, Tuscany, Provence, Sonoma, Oaxaca, Mendoza, Hokkaido, Marlborough, Cape Winelands, Alentejo
- Each property named after region: Acme [Region Name]
- Design incorporates regional architectural cues within modern framework
- Culinary program completely unique to each location—no standardized dishes
- Local staff, local knowledge, local relationships
- Consistent brand promise, distinct regional expression

**Why it matters:**
Sophisticated travelers want to collect experiences across our collection, knowing each property will feel entirely different while maintaining the Acme standard. We're not replicating a formula—we're expressing the same values in wildly different contexts, guided by long-term stewardship rather than short-term scale.

## The Experience Design Pyramid

### Layer 1: Brand DNA (Foundation)
Everything builds from our core belief: exceptional modern architecture + absolute regional provenance + immersive culinary education = transformative luxury hospitality. Every decision must serve this foundation.

### Layer 2: Experience Proposition
**"Stay in architectural beauty, eat from the region, learn from the source, leave transformed."**

This is our promise. It guides every touchpoint, every investment, every hire.

### Layer 3: UX Fundamentals (The Basics Done Brilliantly)

These are non-negotiable table stakes, executed flawlessly:

- **Arrival:** Seamless, impressive first view of architecture, warm personal welcome
- **The Room:** Exceptional bed, beautiful design, excellent work space, stunning bathroom, regional materials
- **The Bed:** Perfect sleep—high thread count linens, ideal firmness, blackout capability, quality pillows
- **The Bathroom:** Spacious, beautifully lit, exceptional shower pressure, regional sustainable products
- **The Food:** This is where we truly distinguish—surprising, seasonal, technically excellent, educational
- **Service:** Knowledgeable, warm, anticipatory, professional without being formal
- **WiFi & Technology:** Flawless throughout property—fast, reliable, easy to connect
- **Work Functionality:** Proper desks, excellent lighting, comfortable chairs, power access, quiet spaces
- **Cleanliness:** Impeccable without being sterile, maintaining natural aesthetic

### Layer 4: Brand Lifts (Distinctive Acme Touches)

These elevate the experience from excellent to distinctively Acme:

- **Arrival Provenance Board:** In reception, today's sourcing map showing active suppliers with distances and names
- **The Room Welcome:** Small plate of regional snacks with full sourcing details—not generic fruit and wine
- **Architectural Details Card:** In room, card explaining the building's design philosophy, architect, materials, sustainable features
- **The Culinary Library:** Collection of regional food books, foraging guides, recipe collections in each property
- **Open Kitchen View:** Multiple vantage points to watch culinary team work throughout the day
- **The Chef's Wall:** Photography and profiles of executive chef and culinary team—guests know who's cooking
- **Daily Foraging Report:** Update each morning on what was found that day, where, and how it's being used
- **Sourcing Transparency:** QR codes on menus linking to supplier profiles, farm photos, ingredient stories
- **Architect-Guided Tours:** Weekly property tours led by staff explaining design decisions and sustainable features
- **The Landscape Map:** Hand-illustrated property and surroundings map showing hiking trails, foraging spots, swimming holes, viewpoints
- **Producer Connections:** Easy ability to buy ingredients directly from suppliers—cheese, honey, preserves, wine

### Layer 5: Signature Moments (The Unforgettable)

These are the experiences guests remember and share. They must be:
- **Relevant:** Connected to our architecture + food + landscape mission
- **Ownable:** Distinctively Acme, impossible to copy without our commitment
- **Visible:** Guests directly experience them
- **Memorable:** Create strong emotional resonance and stories to share
- **Talkable:** Interesting enough to naturally generate word-of-mouth

#### Signature Moment 1: **The Architecture Reveal**

**What it is:** As guests drive toward the property, the architecture is deliberately obscured by landscape until a specific moment when it fully reveals—the building emerging from hillside, sitting above valley, nestled in forest. It's choreographed to create an intake-of-breath moment. Upon arrival, staff welcomes them not just to a hotel, but to a piece of architecture designed specifically for this landscape by [architect name]. A brief orientation shares the design philosophy, sustainable features, and regional materials used.

**Why it works:**
- Immediately signals this isn't a standard hotel
- Creates a memorable first impression worth photographing and sharing
- Positions the building itself as part of the experience, not just background
- Demonstrates our commitment to exceptional design
- Sets expectations: everything here will be thoughtfully considered

**The feeling:** "This building is extraordinary. It belongs here. I can't wait to explore it and photograph it. This stay is going to be special."

#### Signature Moment 2: **The Dawn Foraging Invitation**

**What it is:** Each morning, weather permitting, the chef or forager does a dawn walk through the landscape—forest, meadow, coastline, garden—gathering wild ingredients. Guests are invited to join (optional, small group, 6-8 people maximum, boots and baskets provided). The experience is 60-90 minutes: walking in early morning light, learning to identify wild foods, understanding seasonality and terroir, gathering breakfast ingredients. Return to property to find those ingredients incorporated into breakfast service—often within an hour of picking.

**Why it works:**
- Creates profound connection between landscape and plate
- Offers genuine learning—guests acquire knowledge they take home
- Produces incredible food photography opportunities
- Shows our commitment to regional sourcing in action
- Creates shared experience and conversation among guests
- Varies completely by property, season, weather—never the same twice

**The feeling:** "I just ate mushrooms I picked an hour ago. I learned to identify wild plants. I watched the sun rise over this landscape and understood, viscerally, what 'local food' means. This is extraordinary."

#### Signature Moment 3: **The Chef's Counter Tasting Experience**

**What it is:** Best seats in the house: 8-10 counter seats facing the open kitchen where guests watch evening service while eating an extended tasting menu (10-12 courses). The chef and sous chefs explain each dish as it's plated—not just ingredients, but technique, inspiration, provenance story, why this preparation. It's interactive: guests ask questions, chefs respond, conversation flows. The experience typically lasts 3-4 hours and costs premium pricing (£175-225 / $220-280), but it's the most sought-after reservation.

**Why it works:**
- Offers unprecedented access to culinary craft
- Satisfies food-obsessed guests' desire to understand technique
- Creates intimacy and connection with culinary team
- Produces exceptional food photography (watching dishes being plated)
- Justifies premium pricing through exclusivity and depth
- Generates social media content and word-of-mouth
- Celebrates our transparent, educational approach

**The feeling:** "I just watched a master chef prepare my dinner while explaining every decision. I learned more about cooking in one evening than I have in years. The food was exceptional, but the experience was transformative."

#### Signature Moment 4: **The Regional Provenance Map Room**

**What it is:** A dedicated space (often library or lounge) featuring a large, beautiful map of the region showing every farm, producer, forager, and food source we work with. Each supplier has a pin with photo and story. Guests can explore the map before or after dining, understanding exactly where their food came from. Adjacent: current menu posted with lines connecting each dish to its sources on the map. Interactive tablets allow deeper exploration—farm profiles, ingredient histories, seasonal availability, recipe suggestions.

**Why it works:**
- Makes provenance tangible and visual
- Allows self-directed exploration for curious guests
- Creates incredible photo opportunity (the map is beautifully designed)
- Demonstrates radical transparency
- Sparks conversations among guests
- Educates without requiring staff time for every explanation
- Becomes a landmark within the property—"meet at the map room"

**The feeling:** "I can see exactly where everything comes from. This isn't marketing—it's verifiable reality. I could drive to these farms tomorrow. That level of transparency is refreshing and impressive."

#### Signature Moment 5: **The Hands-On Harvest Experience**

**What it is:** Seasonally appropriate, hands-on participation in actual food production happening at or near the property. Could be:
- Harvesting kitchen garden vegetables for tonight's dinner
- Helping with honey extraction from property beehives
- Participating in olive or grape harvest at adjacent farm
- Learning to make cheese with local producer
- Preserving seasonal glut into jams, pickles, ferments for property use
- Mushroom foraging with expert mycologist

Not performative—guests actually contribute to the food operation. Their harvested vegetables appear on menus. The honey they helped extract is served at breakfast. The preserves they made will be used for months.

**Why it works:**
- Offers genuine participation, not tourism
- Creates pride and ownership—"I helped make that"
- Demonstrates the labor and skill behind food production
- Varies by property and season—unique to when/where you visit
- Produces hands-on memories and skills guests take home
- Authentic enough to generate compelling content and stories

**The feeling:** "I didn't just eat great food—I helped produce it. I got my hands dirty, I learned real skills, and tonight I'll eat vegetables I personally harvested this morning. I'll never forget this."

#### Signature Moment 6: **The Departure Provenance Portfolio**

**What it is:** Upon checkout, guests receive a beautiful small-format book (6" x 8", 30-40 pages, perfect-bound) custom to their stay. Inside:
- Photos of the property and architecture
- Complete provenance list of every ingredient they ate during their stay
- Profiles of 3-4 key suppliers with contact information
- 2-3 signature recipes from their meals, with regional sourcing suggestions for making at home
- Seasonal calendar showing what's available when if they return
- Hand-written note from executive chef

Optional add-on: small package of shelf-stable regional products (honey, preserved items, spices) they can purchase to recreate dishes at home.

**Why it works:**
- Extends the experience beyond the stay—they'll keep this book
- Provides recipes to recreate dishes, maintaining connection
- Enables continued engagement (they can order ingredients, plan return visit seasonally)
- Beautiful physical object worth keeping, not disposable collateral
- Demonstrates the depth of our sourcing and culinary program
- Creates word-of-mouth when guests show the book to friends

**The feeling:** "They gave me recipes to recreate dishes at home, complete sourcing information, and a beautiful book documenting my stay. I can actually continue this experience in my own kitchen. This is incredibly thoughtful."

## Visual Identity Principles

### Color Palette
**Sophisticated Neutrals with Regional Accents**

Our core palette is refined and timeless—allowing the architecture, landscape, and food to provide color rather than competing with it. Regional variations incorporate local palette.

- **Core Neutrals:** Warm limestone, natural linen, charcoal, soft white, natural wood tones
- **Regional Accents (vary by property):** Drawn from local landscape—Cotswolds honey stone, Tuscan terracotta, Sonoma coastal sage, Hokkaido volcanic black
- **Food Photography:** Vibrant and natural—showcasing real ingredient colors without artificial enhancement
- **Never:** Corporate blues, bright primaries, anything artificial or synthetic-feeling

### Typography
**Modern, Refined, Highly Legible**

- **Headlines:** Contemporary serif that feels architectural—strong, elegant, timeless (think Freight or Canela)
- **Body:** Exceptional sans-serif with multiple weights for hierarchy—clean and highly legible (think GT America or Founders Grotesk)
- **Accents:** Occasional serif italic for provenance details, creating contrast
- **Never:** Trendy fonts that will date quickly, overly decorative scripts, anything that impedes readability

### Photography Style
**Architectural, Editorial, Honest**

Our imagery is sophisticated and magazine-quality—but always true to reality:

- **Architecture:** Showcasing buildings in landscape, emphasizing materials and light, shot by professional architectural photographers
- **Food:** Editorial style showing real dishes, beautiful plating, ingredient close-ups, behind-the-scenes kitchen action
- **Landscape:** Showing the regions as they truly are—beautiful but honest, emphasizing agriculture and nature
- **People:** Chefs working, guests learning, foragers teaching—authentic moments, never staged poses
- **Process:** Hands preparing food, ingredients being harvested, techniques being demonstrated
- **Details:** Material textures, architectural details, ingredient close-ups showing quality

**Never:**
- Overly retouched or artificial-looking imagery
- Generic stock photography
- Forced lifestyle shots that don't reflect reality
- Photography that feels influencer-driven rather than editorial

### Material Language

In physical spaces, materials communicate quality, sustainability, and regional character:

- **Architectural Materials:** Stone, wood, glass, concrete sourced regionally where possible
- **Furnishings:** Natural fibers—linen, wool, leather—in neutral tones
- **Tableware:** Handmade ceramics from regional potters, handblown glassware, quality flatware
- **Finishes:** Natural and honest—we show the material, not cover it with paint or veneer
- **Texture:** Layered and tactile—rough stone against smooth wood, soft textiles against hard surfaces
- **Art:** Regional artists and makers, often commissioned specifically for property

**What we avoid:**
- Synthetic materials that look plastic or cheap
- Overly polished finishes that feel corporate
- Trend-driven design that will date quickly
- Anything that feels mass-produced rather than considered

## Service Standards

### The Acme Way

Our service philosophy balances sophistication with warmth, knowledge with accessibility, professionalism with personality.

**Deep Knowledge, Effortless Delivery**
Every team member understands our regional provenance philosophy intimately. They can discuss suppliers, ingredients, foraging, seasonal availability, architectural details, local hiking trails. But they share this knowledge conversationally, not pedantically. Guests feel educated, never lectured.

**Anticipation Through Observation**
We notice what guests need before they ask. The extra layer when afternoon temperature drops. The quiet table when they're working over lunch. The gluten-free accommodation made seamlessly. The recommendation for tomorrow's hike based on their fitness level observed today.

**Professional Warmth**
We're genuinely glad guests are here, and it shows. We make eye contact, we smile, we use names, we remember preferences. But we maintain appropriate professional boundaries—warm, not familiar. Attentive, not intrusive.

**Family-Led Hosting**
Guests should feel welcomed by people who will be here for the long haul. We emphasize continuity, long tenure, and personal accountability, creating the feeling of returning to a trusted home rather than a rotating staff.

**Transparent Communication**
When regional sourcing creates constraints, we explain honestly. When something goes wrong, we own it immediately and fix it generously. We don't make corporate excuses or hide behind policy.

**Empowered Problem-Solving**
Staff have authority to make decisions that improve guest experience without seeking approval. Move a room, comp an experience, adjust a dish, extend a checkout. Trust creates better service than rigid protocols.

**Excellence as Baseline**
Great service isn't noteworthy—it's expected. What distinguishes Acme is the depth: staff who can answer complex questions about food sourcing, explain architectural features, recommend precisely the right wine, adjust experiences based on individual interests.

## What Makes Acme, Acme?

**It's the architecture.**
We don't build cookie-cutter hotels. We commission significant contemporary architects to design buildings that honor landscape, use regional materials, and create profound spatial experiences. The architecture itself is a reason to visit.

**It's the absolute provenance.**
We're not loosely "farm-to-table." We source 100% of ingredients from the region—auditable, transparent, verifiable. Every dish tells you exactly where it came from. This isn't marketing. It's our operational reality.

**It's the culinary immersion.**
We don't just feed you—we teach you. Open kitchens, chef interactions, foraging walks, cooking classes, producer visits. You leave understanding the region's food culture, not just having consumed it.

**It's the small global collection.**
Each property is completely distinct—different architecture, different landscape, different cuisine—united by shared values. You can collect experiences across continents, always knowing you'll find exceptional design and regional food, never knowing exactly what form it will take. The collection stays intentionally small, stewarded for the long term.

**It's the sophistication without pretense.**
We're serious about craft—architectural, culinary, agricultural—but never stuffy. We create beautiful spaces and exceptional food that invite joy, curiosity, and wonder. Luxury should feel good, not intimidating.

**It's the integration of work and pleasure.**
We recognize how modern travelers actually live. You can take a morning call from a beautiful room, work from the library after lunch, then forage at sunset and dine exceptionally. We don't make you choose between productivity and restoration—we enable both.

**It's the proof.**
Everything we claim, we can demonstrate. Visit the farms. Meet the chef. Tour the architecture. Join the foraging walk. We have nothing to hide and everything to share. That radical transparency builds trust and creates loyalty.

**Most of all, it's the transformation.**

Guests arrive seeking a beautiful place to eat and sleep. They leave having learned to forage, understanding terroir, appreciating modern architecture, and feeling profoundly connected to a landscape they barely knew. That transformation—from tourist to student to advocate—is what we create.
